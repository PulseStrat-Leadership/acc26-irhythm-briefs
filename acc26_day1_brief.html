What happened: CHAMPION-AF enrolled 3,000 NVAF patients, randomized to WATCHMAN FLX vs. NOACs. At three years: 45% reduction in non-procedural bleeding (10.9% vs. 19.0%; P<0.001) with non-inferior efficacy. Published NEJM simultaneously. BSX is submitting for expanded first-line LAAC coverage. Over 600,000 WATCHMAN implants to date.

Why iRhythm should care: This trial fundamentally expands who is eligible for LAAC — from patients who can\u2019t tolerate anticoagulation to patients who simply prefer a device over lifelong medication (an estimated 40% of AF patients are non-adherent to prescribed blood thinners). Every patient who enters the LAAC pathway requires: (1) documented AF burden to justify the procedure, (2) pre-procedural extended ambulatory monitoring to characterize AF pattern and confirm candidacy, and (3) post-procedural monitoring to assess residual AF and guide the anticoagulation weaning protocol. The Zio platform is the clinical-grade monitoring standard for all three of these touchpoints.

🔨 What iRhythm Should Do

(1) Develop a “LAAC Pathway Monitoring Protocol” positioning Zio as the standard pre- and post-WATCHMAN monitoring platform — co-market with BSX and EP labs performing LAAC. (2) Quantify the market sizing opportunity: if CHAMPION-AF doubles the LAAC-eligible population from ~250K to ~500K annually in the US, and each patient needs 2–3 Zio monitoring episodes across the LAAC journey, this represents 500K–1.5M incremental Zio patches per year at maturity. (3) Brief the payor relations team: expanded LAAC coverage will require monitoring documentation for prior authorization — Zio reporting should be integrated into the LAAC PA workflow. POTS
NIH RECOVER Initiative · RECOVER-AUTONOMIC (Ivabradine) · Featured Session · 12:00 PM CST
POTS Trial\u2019s Key Finding: Coordinated Monitoring + Care Coordination Is the Therapeutic Unlock — Continuous Ambulatory ECG Is the Delivery Mechanism for the Model That Worked
High AdjacencyRPM · New SegmentCare Coordination ⚠ Mixed · Opportunity SignalMar 28 · ACC.26 Featured
▼

What happened: Largest-ever POTS RCT (181 patients, 85% female, median age 39). Ivabradine reduced HR significantly but did NOT meet the primary symptom endpoint. Critical finding: the combination of ivabradine + coordinated care (weekly care coordinator calls, dietary guidance, compression, structured activity) showed statistically significant benefit (P=.004 interaction). Up to 31% of post-COVID infection patients may develop POTS. Blood samples stored for biomarker-driven responder identification.

Why iRhythm should care: The trial\u2019s most actionable conclusion is that the care coordination model — not the drug alone — is what unlocks clinical benefit in POTS. That care coordination model requires: continuous heart rate monitoring, structured symptom tracking, and weekly data-informed check-ins. This is precisely what an extended ambulatory ECG monitoring platform enables. POTS affects an estimated 1–3 million Americans, a number that has grown post-COVID. This is an entirely new clinical segment for cardiac monitoring that did not exist in meaningful scale before COVID-19.

🔨 What iRhythm Should Do

(1) Commission a clinical development assessment: can Zio\u2019s 14-day continuous ECG data support POTS diagnosis (tilt-table equivalent or complementary), symptom-HR correlation, and treatment response monitoring? (2) Engage the RECOVER-AUTONOMIC investigators (Dr. Pam Taub, UCSD) about a follow-on pilot using Zio monitoring as the continuous data backbone for the coordinated care intervention. (3) Brief the health economics team: POTS patients currently cycle through 7+ specialists before diagnosis (average 4-year diagnostic odyssey) — a Zio-based screening pathway could compress this dramatically and create a strong value-based care argument. (4) Monitor the biomarker analysis: if specific phenotypes predict ivabradine + monitoring response, this creates a precision monitoring use case for Zio AI.

Deep Analysis · What Changed on Day 1 & What It Means for iRhythm
🟢 The AI Translation Narrative Is Now Owned by iRhythm

Before Day 1, “AI in cardiology” was a generic talking point shared by dozens of companies. After Turakhia\u2019s Bishop Keynote, the narrative has been personalized: iRhythm\u2019s Zio is THE reference implementation. The specific framing matters: Turakhia didn\u2019t argue that AI has potential (old news). He argued that translation — safe, equitable, reimbursed, scaled deployment — is the hard part, and Zio is the proof that it can be done. This positions iRhythm as the “adult in the room” while consumer-grade competitors (Apple Watch, AliveCor) are implicitly positioned as science experiments without a clinical delivery model.

The “de-skilling” concern Turakhia raised is a sophisticated competitive weapon: it pre-empts the argument that fully automated AI (no clinician review) is better. Zio\u2019s clinician-in-the-loop model is framed as a feature, not a limitation.

📈 CHAMPION-AF Creates the Biggest Single-Day Demand Signal for Ambulatory Cardiac Monitoring in 2026

The math is straightforward. Today, approximately 150,000–200,000 WATCHMAN procedures are performed annually in the US. CHAMPION-AF\u2019s data — if it drives expanded first-line coverage — could double or triple the eligible population over 3–5 years. Each LAAC candidate needs: (1) baseline ambulatory monitoring to document AF burden, (2) monitoring during the post-implant anticoagulation weaning period (45–90 days), and (3) long-term surveillance monitoring to detect residual AF. Conservative estimate: 2–3 Zio monitoring episodes per LAAC patient journey.

The non-adherence argument is equally important: the 40% of AF patients who are non-adherent to prescribed blood thinners are the clearest target for LAAC. These are also the patients most likely to have undiagnosed or undocumented AF patterns — exactly the population where Zio\u2019s 14-day continuous monitoring is most clinically valuable.

🩹 POTS/Long COVID Represents an Entirely New Clinical Segment for Cardiac Monitoring

The RECOVER-AUTONOMIC finding — that coordinated care + monitoring is the therapeutic unlock — is the most important signal for iRhythm\u2019s TAM expansion thesis. POTS affects an estimated 1–3 million Americans, with numbers growing post-COVID. These patients are: young (median age 39), predominantly female (85%), high healthcare utilizers (7+ specialists before diagnosis), and currently monitored with inadequate tools (spot vitals, tilt-table tests). Continuous ambulatory ECG monitoring captures the exact data (HR variability, positional HR changes, arrhythmia burden) that POTS management requires.

The economic argument is compelling: the average POTS patient costs the healthcare system $40,000+ in unnecessary ER visits, specialist consultations, and misdiagnosis before receiving a correct diagnosis. A Zio-based screening pathway could compress this dramatically.

🧬 The Platform Evolution Story: From Arrhythmia Detection to Cardiovascular Risk Intelligence

The dyslipidemia guideline\u2019s validation of AI-enabled risk stratification tools is a strategic signal for iRhythm\u2019s 24-month product roadmap. Published AI-ECG literature demonstrates that single-lead and multi-lead ECG data can predict: left ventricular hypertrophy, low ejection fraction, hyperkalemia, valvular disease signatures, and metabolic risk markers. Zio\u2019s 14-day dataset is the richest ambulatory ECG data source in clinical medicine — it contains signals that have never been systematically extracted.

The strategic question: should iRhythm invest in expanding the Zio AI platform from a single-purpose arrhythmia detector to a multi-signal cardiovascular risk platform? The TAM expansion from arrhythmia monitoring (~$5B globally) to cardiovascular risk stratification (~$20B+) is the most compelling long-term equity story for IRTC. ACC.26\u2019s emphasis on AI translation provides the clinical validation environment to begin this pivot.
